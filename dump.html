TRACE A discount tracking app for clothing.

In 2023, the global fashion industry will need to weather inflation while finding opportunities in shifting consumer patterns, channel and digital marketing strategies, and manufacturing approaches.

Reducing Clothing Waste

This year’s report presents a difficult outlook ahead, as fashion companies face challenges and revise forecasts downward after an exceptionally strong 2021, per McKinsey analysis of global data in the fashion industry. Inflation and geopolitical concerns dominate the agenda for 2023, negatively affecting both consumer demand and brands’ operating costs. Consumers are adjusting their behaviors, as many trade down to cheaper or discounted items to reduce their spending, though the luxury sector will remain strong, with affluent consumers less heavily affected by inflation.

Fashion companies that can adapt to the increasing complexity by updating their operating models and adjusting their strategies for supply chain, sales channels, and digital marketing will be best placed to weather the upcoming storm. They can lean into the following ten emerging consumer trends.

Key Features

TRACE is a mobile discount tracking app that focuses not only on tracking,but also on helping users to make purchasing descisions more easily.

User Research

Surveys and interviews showed that people encountered numerous points of difficulty when they attempted to buy items on sale.

Making people aware that there is a sale going on is simply the first hurdle amongst many. The higher the value the more competition, and the price can fluctuate unpredictably. There is also the element of time sensitvity: different issues arise at different points of the purchasing timeline.

Key Frames

Homepage

The homepage follows a newsfeed format where you can find new updates and most often used functions. The top section is a swipeable newsfeed that shows new and upcoming store sales, the bottom section contains new updates sorted into categories called 'highlights'.
